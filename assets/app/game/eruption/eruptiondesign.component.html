Eruption Design

Gameplay

The design of Eruption is focussed on making a short, interesting, puzzle game with as much replayability as possible. Several decisions were made to make sure that (especially in speed mode) the game is simple enough to be played very fast, but with enough difficulty to make it interesting. Both modes are simply scored by how far the lava gets without a city being destoyed, the points are representative of the lowest lava position.

Lava movement

Left, right, and split trenches

Timer, rocks, and a city

The tiling was first prototyped using squares but hexagonal tiles provide a much better, and clearer lava movement dynamic. The hexagonal interlocking pattern can clearly show that the lava has a chance to move left or right when it moves to the next row. This also cleared up the movement design of the lava, as the options for movement were reduced to left, right, or split. The cylinder of hexagons is procedurally generated as the player progresses, with each tile having a specific chance of being set as rock, water, or city. Rocks are very important as they provide obstacles, and without these obstacles the game becomes too simple, with there even being a possibility of redirecting all the lava to a single stream and endlessly continuing the game, so by adding rock tiles where the player cannot build some more thought has to be used to place the trenches. In a similar way to the rocks providing extra obstacles, the water provides targets. When the lava hits a water tile it is slowed down, so that it's movement takes twice as long through that tile, giving the player longer to decide on their next move.

Game Modes

The game includes a speed mode and a strategy mode. Details of each are covered below. I chose to have 2 modes as during the design and testing I found that changing the speed of the lava had a great effect on the gameplay. The faster the lava the more frantic and exciting the gameplay became, and the slower the lava the less interesting and exciting. However when testing the slower lava I saw the possibility of making the game more strategic, limiting resources for the player and adding additional events during the game. So while the speed mode provides a faster more exciting game of simply diverting the lava as fast as you can, the strategy mode provides something more in depth and thoughtful. I think there is enough difference in the game modes and the experience each provides that both are worthwhile additions.

Speed Mode

Speed mode is the simpler of the two game modes, there is no limit on building and the trenches are completed immediately. However, as per the name, the lava in speed mode is faster than the lava in strategy mode and the player must constantly be on the move, checking which cities are in the most danger and planning ahead as much as possible. The lava speeds up as the game goes on, and the layout of the tiles becomes more difficult to overcome (more rocks, more cities). The lava in the lowest position is shown by a ring of darkened tiles around the map so that the player can see if they are dealing with the most advanced lava (and if not they still have to find it). Overall speed mode is frantic and tense that hopefully gives players a quick burst of fun gameplay.

Strategy Mode

Strategy mode has more gameplay features than speed mode, this is mainly allowed by the slower speed of the lava. In strategy mode the number of builders is limited to 5, and the time that each trench takes to build is dependent on the ground it is built upon. The screen is a little busier as it must accomodate more features, however it does not feel too crowded. There are also events that take place at set intervals, these can provide a variety of effects, from slowing the lava, collapsing trenches, and creating new lava streams, to halving build times, or incapacitating one builder.

Builders

Event timer

Ground status

The builders UI counts down the time until each builder has completed the current trench, the time that this takes for each tile can be seen in the ground status bar, along with the currently selected trench direction. If the player can plan well they can mainly build on the softer ground as their builders will be able to get more done. If all 5 builders are busy no new trenches can be built until one becomes free

The event timer counts down until each event. The event is randomly chosen from 6

Eruption - new streams of lava are added and the lava is a little faster for a short time

Flood - The lava is slowed but some trenches collapse, releasing the lava again

Heavy rain - Building times for trenches are slower

Trench collapse - Some trenches collapse releasing lava again

Overtime - Builders complete trenches faster

Injury - One builder is incapacitated

I used this variety and number of events as there is a good balance of positive and negative effects and I found that each in game event could be based on a logical real world event to make them more recognisable.

Audio and Visual

The art of the game is simple with a low poly count, I find that this can help to easily allow the player to quickly identify what is on the map as there are only 2 extra features beside colour, and the sharp straight edges of the city is easily recognisable when compared to the round shape of the rocks. The colours of the tiles denote the hardness of the ground (build time in strategy mode, only decorative in speed mode). I added more definition to the trenches and cities, and also added some effects to make the lava even more visible.

The music adds to the tense atmosphere, and the sounds should be easily identifiable to show what is happening both visually and audibly. There is a small sound that occurs when the lava moves and another when trenches complete, giving more cues to the player to understand what is happening. Each event has a small sound (or a larger sound for eruption), and there is a sound that accompanies game over.

Throughout the game I reuse the hexagons (as game objects, and menu items), to try to keep a running theme in the game and provide some overarching cohesion.

A game just starting out, with updated city models

Overall I think that the gameplay is successful, some tweaking could be made to the trench building options, and perhaps the scoring system to give bonuses for certain trench layouts or 'combos'. I think that the look of the game works well, it keeps the simple style but has a bit of definition in the models of the cities (as can be seen from the improvement between the earlier city image and the cities in the gameplay image) and the trenches. The music works quite well and the newer sound effects are a great improvement over the old ones.
